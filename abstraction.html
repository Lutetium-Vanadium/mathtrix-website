Abstraction

Mathematics is the purest of the arts, as well as the most misunderstood. Paul Lockhart

Through Abstraction we hope to do justice to this fallen art. This event draws inspiration from the worlds of logic and creativity and seeks to explore the realm of mathematical art. Artistic skill and problem-solving abilities are prime characteristics to help participants in this innovative event. Abstraction welcomes artists to transform art pieces like The Starry Night, The Last Supper or the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel or create their own to express themselves. Artists are welcome to test their creativity and solve problems to create a masterpiece!

Rules

  • Each team must consist of two participants - one artist and one question solver (preferably aware of 12th math).
  • This event has only one round.
  • The question solver unlocks rgb codes of colours by answering some questions.
  • The artist must make an abstract piece depicting given math topics while using colours unlocked by the question solver only.
  • The final piece to be submitted along with a 50 word write up explaining the art piece. Answers to the questions asked in relation to the RGB codes must also be submitted.
